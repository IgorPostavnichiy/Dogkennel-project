We hope we can work together!

To adopt a Doberman, all children in the home must be age seven (7) or older. Our goal is to maximize the chances of success for every adoption, and our experience has shown us that this is best accomplished when all children in the family are emotionally and physically mature enough to properly interact with a large dog. This policy is in place to ensure the safety and well-being of both our rescue dogs and our adoptive families. Other breeds and cats have more flexibility for childrens ages.

Health

We often hear heart-breaking tales of beloved Dobermans who have suffered early deaths due to genetically inheritable diseases. While there is never a guarantee, even with mechanical and physical testing, performing these procedures on our dogs provides the highest level of certainty for healthy production.

Temperament

Our Doberman puppies are raised with the ideal family companion in mind. We use early socialization alongside a variety of harmless stimuli to promote sound, reliable temperaments in all our pups. These include a variety of people, noises, situations, and other animals.

Breed Standard

Here at The Kennel Birkline, we breed with the ideal Doberman Pinscher standard in mind, and adhere to recommendations issued by both the American Kennel Club and the United Kennel Club. There are minor differences between them; mainly height and temperament.

Dog Champions

Our dogs participate in international dog shows FCI, they regularly take prizes and stand out among the others for their beauty, temperament, work and the ability to win! That is why dogs from our kennel are very much in demand and appreciated all over the world.
